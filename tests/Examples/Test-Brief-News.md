# Daily Ukraine War Brief – 11 November 2025

As of **11 November 2025**, the day’s Ukraine war picture is shaped by three main strands: intensified Russian ground advances in the east, escalating mutual strikes on energy and fuel infrastructure, and sharpening political, sanctions and governance currents around the conflict.

---

## 1. Frontline: Pressure on Pokrovsk, Kupiansk & the eastern arc

Russian forces are pushing hardest along the Donetsk and Kharkiv axes, seeking their most significant territorial gains since 2023.

- **Pokrovsk**: Russian units have entered parts of the city and surrounding settlements, which Moscow frames as a strategic “gateway” towards Kramatorsk and Sloviansk. Visual evidence indicates Russian troops present but not in full control. Ukraine disputes claims of encirclement, insisting supply routes to nearby Myrnohrad remain open and counterattacks ongoing.
- **Myrnohrad**: Russia portrays the town as nearly enveloped; Ukraine says its forces are still resisting. Independent confirmation is limited; the situation remains fluid.
- **Kupiansk (Kharkiv region)**: Russian forces claim control over the eastern part of Kupiansk and continued advances toward key rail and logistics nodes. Independent assessments broadly confirm Russian tactical gains but not all official Russian claims.

Analysts note that while these gains are relatively limited, they increase pressure on Ukrainian logistics and could complicate the defence of the wider Donbas arc if Pokrovsk or adjoining hubs become untenable.

---

## 2. Air war & infrastructure: Energy and logistics under attack

Both sides intensified long-range strikes, targeting each other’s energy and economic infrastructure.

### Russian strikes on Ukraine

- Renewed waves of missiles and drones have hit power and energy facilities, contributing to further damage to the grid and rolling blackouts affecting millions as winter approaches.
- The attacks reinforce Ukraine’s calls for additional Western air defence systems, especially Patriot and other high-end interceptors.

### Ukrainian strikes on Russia & occupied territory

- Ukrainian drones and long-range strikes targeted fuel and port infrastructure in Russia and occupied Crimea, including a major Black Sea oil and export hub and an oil depot in occupied territory.
- Russia reports intercepting many drones and accuses Ukraine of targeting civilian sites; several claims remain unverified.

The pattern on both sides underscores a strategy of undermining each other’s energy resilience, logistics, and economic capacity, with heightened concerns about spillover along NATO’s borders after recurring drone debris incidents.

---

## 3. Politics, diplomacy & sanctions: Confrontation over compromise

Diplomatic signalling continues to point to entrenched positions rather than active movement toward a negotiated settlement.

- The Kremlin reiterates that it “wants the war to end” while maintaining conditions that remain unacceptable to Kyiv, leaving no credible peace track in play.
- The United States has hardened its posture, with continued or expanded sanctions affecting Russian energy and finance sectors, aimed at constraining Moscow’s ability to fund the war over the long term.
- Within Europe, institutions are moving to bolster security, intelligence sharing, and resilience against Russian-linked threats, reflecting the war’s long-term impact on European strategic architecture.

Overall, the political environment remains one of sustained confrontation with limited diplomatic off-ramps visible.

---

## 4. Inside Ukraine: Resilience and governance

Domestic messaging from Kyiv highlights both resilience on the front and efforts to maintain Western confidence.

- President Volodymyr Zelenskyy’s visit to front-line or recently liberated areas serves to signal commitment to contested regions under persistent Russian fire.
- Ukrainian authorities have announced further investigations and charges in corruption and defence-related cases, part of a broader effort to demonstrate accountability, reassure citizens, and meet expectations from international partners tying long-term support to governance reforms.

These moves are designed to reinforce both domestic morale and external trust at a time of mounting battlefield and economic pressure.

---

## 5. Strategic picture in brief

1. **Russian tactical momentum** around Pokrovsk, Myrnohrad, and Kupiansk is testing Ukrainian lines and seeking an exploitable breakthrough.
2. **Ukrainian asymmetric strikes** on Russian and occupied infrastructure raise costs for Moscow and signal that deep rear areas are not immune.
3. **Energy and infrastructure warfare** is intensifying, with significant humanitarian implications for Ukraine and economic implications for Russia.
4. **Diplomatic space is constrained**, with sanctions, security measures, and hardened rhetoric outweighing any movement toward talks.
5. **Kyiv’s focus on resilience and anti-corruption** is central to sustaining Western support through another difficult winter phase of the war.
